Distribution Plans

Craig Kerstiens is an early member of the Heroku team. He blogs frequently about Python, Django and Postgres and curates content for Postgres Weekly and Postgres Guide.

Timing Your Launches

Have your content ready and schedule a time for launch. When should you launch?

  • Monday is not a good day for launching. You’re up all weekend preparing for a Monday launch. This creates problems internally and your launch will be affected.
  • Tuesday through Thursday are great. “If we can get it on Hacker News' number one spot on Tuesday, we'll have this many page views and we're going to have this many sign-ups, and that's great,” Kerstiens says.
  • Fridays are good for smaller products. “If Twitter volume is busier on Tuesday, but quieter on Friday, and if I can launch a tier two or tier three, a smaller product update, I can get eyeballs that I wouldn't otherwise get. Other people are competing for those, so this is a really nice opportunity.”
  • Weekends work for posts. “For smaller, interesting engineering blog posts, thought leadership, etc, it's really easy to do. Weekends are quiet, but there's still developers reading and engaging with your content on the weekends.”
